My borewell bill keeps climbing. Should I water longer, or more often?

10:45 AM
smart_toy

On your sandy loam, water takes in fast and holds poorly. Increase frequency rather than duration.

science Why this matters

Long irrigation sets push water below the root zone. You pay to pump it, the crop never uses it, and it carries nitrogen down with it. Shorter, more frequent cycles hold moisture where the roots actually are.

Three 12-minute cycles, four hours apart, suits your soil and crop stage. Across six acres that is roughly ₹3,100 a month off your power bill. Shall I cost it out properly?
